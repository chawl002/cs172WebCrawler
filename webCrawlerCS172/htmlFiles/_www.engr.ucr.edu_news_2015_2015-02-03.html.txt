      <div class="clear section"></div> 
      <p>Engineering seniors Joel Sanchez and Claire Tran recently received top awards for their research presented at the Annual Biomedical Research Conference for Minority Students (ABRCMS) in San Antonio, Texas. The Bourns College of Engineering undergraduates were among 17 students nationwide whose work was honored as outstanding in their disciplines of engineering, physics, and mathematics.<br><br>Sanchez's presentation was entitled "Heterologous expression of antimycins in E. coli." and presented the findings of the function of specific modification enzymes in the antimycin biosynthetic pathway. This research can facilitate the optimization of metabolite yield and pave the way for future antimycin derivatives with a wide arrange of bioactivities.</p> 
      <p>Tran's presentation was called "Designing an in vitro bistable biological circuit using RNA aptamers" and explored the design of a synthetic biochemical switch. The future impact of this research can range from controlling gene expression to improving nanoscale biological circuits.<br><br>More than 1,700 students participated in poster and oral presentations in 12 subdisciplines of the biomedical and behavioral sciences, with their work being evaluated by active-researcher scientists. The highest scoring undergraduate researchers earned monetary awards. The ABRCMS is one of the largest professional conferences of its kind in the nation and is designed to encourage underrepresented minority students and students with disabilities to pursue advanced training in the biomedical and behavioral sciences, including STEM disciplines.<br><br>The ABRCMS is sponsored by the Division of Minority Opportunity Research, National Institute of General Medical Sciences of the National Institutes for Health.<br><br>To congratulate Joel and Claire, visit the <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/Bourns-College-of-Engineering/118503574842498">Bourns College of Engineering Facebook page</a>.</p>
