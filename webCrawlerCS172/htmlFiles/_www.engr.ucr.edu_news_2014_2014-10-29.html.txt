      </div> 
      <p>RIVERSIDE, Calif. (<a href="http://www.ucr.edu/">www.ucr.edu</a>)&nbsp;— The University of California, Riverside <a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.engr.ucr.edu/']);">Bourns College of Engineering</a> hosted the <a href="http://www.iom3.org/content/world-lecture-competition" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.iom3.org/content/world-lecture-competition']);">2014 IOM3 (Institute of Materials, Minerals, and Mining) Young Persons’ World Lecture Competition Final</a> on Oct. 23.</p> 
      <p>The following students took the top spots:</p> 
      <p>* First, Raphael Smith, South Africa, “The design, construction and testing of a hermetically sealed breast platform for dual-modality mammography.</p> 
      <p>* Second, David Bishop, University of Ulster, Ireland, “Addressing an unmet need in von Willebrand disease: A biosurface solution.”</p> 
      <p>* Third, Losini Amarasan, Multimedia University, Malaysia, “Silica fibre: The next material for thermoluminescence dosimeter (TLD).”</p> 
      <p>Ten finalists took part, including Corey Hardin, who is working on his Ph.D. with <a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/faculty/me/garay.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.engr.ucr.edu/faculty/me/garay.html']);">Javier Garay</a>, a professor of mechanical engineering at the Bourns College of Engineering. His talk was called “CAPAD&nbsp;processing of rare earth doped zirconia for high temperature light emission applications.”</p> 
      <p>“This is the first time the Bourns College of Engineering has hosted an international competition like this,” said <a href="http://www.engr.ucr.edu/faculty/me/rabba.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.engr.ucr.edu/faculty/me/rabba.html']);">Reza Abbashian</a>, dean of the college who also served as one of the judges of the competition. “It shows our increasing international stature as a college.”</p> 
      <p>The Young Persons’ World Lecture Competition (YPWLC) has been organized and held annually by the Institute of Materials, Minerals and Mining since 2005. The competition is an extension of the&nbsp;UK Young Persons’ Lecture Competition, and is organized by the&nbsp;Younger Members’ Committee.</p>
