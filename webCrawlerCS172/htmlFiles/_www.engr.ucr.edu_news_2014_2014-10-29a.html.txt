      <p>By night, Shah Selbe is able to draw a standing room only crowd of engineering students eager to hear about his exploits with hippos, elephants, and hyenas while working on impactful projects through his volunteer work with Engineers Without Borders and as an Explorer with the National Geographic Society. By day, he is a satellite propulsion systems engineer at Boeing Space and Intelligence Systems. Whatever the time, or time zone, Shah Selbe '04 is a Bourns College of Engineering (BCOE) alumnus who is on his way to adventure.<br><br>Selbe was recently awarded the 2014 Outstanding Young Alumnus award by the University of California, Riverside Alumni Association, which honors graduates whose personal and professional achievements bring distinction to UCR, contribute to the betterment of society, and enhance their communities.<br><br><img src="/images/Shah%20Selbe%20%20Young%20Alumnus%20award.jpg" alt="Shah Selbe Young Alumnus Award" width="422" height="252"><br><br>The energetic Selbe is one of a handful of gifted and inspiring young professionals whose endeavors have been acknowledged by National Geographic as making a difference--his work as a volunteer on ocean conservation issues with such organizations as the Center for Ocean Solutions and the Monterey Bay Aquarium won him recognition by NatGeo as one of their 2013 Emerging Explorers. As a conservation technologist, Selbe created FishNET, a platform approach harnessing technology to empower coastal communities and detect and track illegal, unreported, and unregulated fishing worldwide.</p> 
      <p>Selbe, who earned his B.S. in Chemical Engineering at BCOE, returned to campus this fall for the alumni award and also visited on Oct. 22 to regale more than 80 current BCOE students with his Engineers Without Borders and National Geographic Expedition tales as part of the distinguished alumni speaker series hosted by BCOE's Student Development and International Initiatives office.&nbsp;</p> 
      <p>Selbe credits BCOE with "(helping) shape me in being the engineer and global citizen I am today."</p> 
      <p><em>View the 2014 Outstanding Young Alumnus Award video: <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TqeQwJSaWnQ">https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TqeQwJSaWnQ</a>.</em></p>
