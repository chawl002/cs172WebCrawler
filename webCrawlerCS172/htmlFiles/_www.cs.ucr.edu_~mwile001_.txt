	<h3>Detecting protein-RNA interaction in MEDLINE</h3>
	<h4>Overview:</h4>
	<p> The goal of this project is to automatically detect <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNA-binding_protein">protein RNA interactions</a> mentioned in the abstracts of citations in <a target="_blank" href="http://www.nlm.nih.gov/bsd/pmresources.html">MEDLINE</a>, with an objective to build a <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein-RNA_interface_database">protein-RNA interaction database</a>.  MEDLINE is a large corpus of approximately <a target="_blank" href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/sites/entrez?Db=journals&Cmd=DetailsSearch&Term=currentlyindexed[All]">5,592</a> journals.  Most of these journals are out of the scope of protein RNA interactions; a biochemist who studies these types of interactions has provided me with the following <a target="_blank" href="list.html">list of journals</a>, which will be used to subset the MEDLINE corpus.</p>
<h4>Motivation</h4>
<p>
In 2010, nearly <a target="_blank" href="http://www.nlm.nih.gov/bsd/index_stats_comp.html">700,000</a> citations were added to MEDLINE, with over 18 million citations in total.  While many of these citations are not concerned with protein-RNA interactions, keeping up with current protein-RNA interactions is still an <a href="list.html" target="_blank">intractable problem</a> without any type of indexing scheme associated with the MEDLINE citations.  Furthermore, current protein RNA interaction databases only look at biological properties of proteins and RNA for identifying these interactions.  This motivates the need for an automated database of protein-RNA interactions based on the literature published in MEDLINE.  
</p>
<h4>Related Work</h4>
<p>There are two types of related work, NLP techniques for identifying semantic relations and databases of protein RNA interaction.  In Biodmedical text analysis, identifying protein-protein interactions has been a focused point of research for over <a target="_blank" href="http://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=protein+protein+interaction+NLP&hl=en&btnG=Search&as_sdt=1%2C5&as_sdtp=on">10 years</a>. 
</p>
<p>
To my current knowledge, there are only 2 other protein-RNA interaction databases currently available: <a target="_blank" href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19357098">BIPA</a> and <a target="_blank" href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3013700/">PRIDB</a>.
</p>
